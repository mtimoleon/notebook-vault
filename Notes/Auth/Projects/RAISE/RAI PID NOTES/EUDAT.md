**EUDAT**, the European Data Infrastructure, offers a range of services to make research data **FAIR** (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) across borders and disciplines. While EUDAT does provide customized solutions for institutions and communities, there are **free services** available for individual researchers, citizen scientists, and policy makers:

1. **Register for EUDAT Services**:
    - Start by **registering for an EUDAT account** if you don’t already have one.
    - EUDAT’s **B2ACCESS** allows users to create credentials using existing academic institutional credentials, social accounts, or email addresses.
2. **Prepare Your Data**:
    - Begin with the **metadata**. Ensure you have enough information about your research data to describe it effectively.
    - Metadata should include details about the dataset, creators, keywords, licensing, and other relevant information.
    - EUDAT’s **B2SHARE** instance provides fields for rich metadata during data sharing, including guidance on selecting the appropriate license.
3. **Store Your Data**:
    - Utilize **EUDAT’s B2DROP data storage services** to upload your research data.
    - B2DROP offers a **free 20GB account** for storing and synchronizing research data.
    - It’s a secure service that allows data exchange, synchronization, and sharing with colleagues and team members.
4. **Data Access and Sharing**:
    - Associate your metadata with your research data.
    - From **B2DROP** or directly through **B2SHARE**, EUDAT provides functionalities for creating and managing metadata associated with your datasets.
